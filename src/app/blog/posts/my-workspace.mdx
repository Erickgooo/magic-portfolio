## My Setup — Built for the Work I Actually Do

Behind every automation system, creative asset, and AI workflow is an environment designed to support it. This isn't about aesthetics — it's about removing friction between an idea and its execution.

Here's a look at how my workspace is structured, and why each piece of it matters.

## The Machine

I work across video editing, AI-powered content production, automation systems, and large creative files — often simultaneously. The hardware needs to keep up without becoming a bottleneck.

- **CPU:** AMD Ryzen 9 9900X
- **GPU:** NVIDIA RTX 4070 Ti Super
- **RAM:** 32GB DDR5 @ 6000MHz
- **Setup:** Dual-monitor setup

The RTX 4070 Ti Super handles DaVinci Resolve color grading and AI-assisted rendering without dropping frames. The Ryzen 9 9900X keeps multitasking fluid when I'm running editing software, browsers, automation tools, and AI platforms at the same time. 32GB of DDR5 at 6000MHz means large project files and heavy asset libraries don't slow down the workflow.

The dual-monitor setup is non-negotiable for the way I work — one screen for active production, one for reference, documentation, or communication. Context-switching without physically switching windows is a small thing that compounds into hours saved.

## Audio & Video

For voiceovers and on-camera content, I use a **Rode NT1** microphone and a **Logitech Brio 4K** webcam. The NT1 is a studio-grade condenser mic — it captures clean, professional audio without the need for post-production cleanup on every take. The Brio 4K handles video quality at a level that doesn't undermine the production value of the surrounding content.

When the voiceover isn't recorded live, I use **ElevenLabs** for AI-generated voice — which pairs with the Rode NT1 workflow depending on the project's needs and timeline.

## The Physical Environment

The space itself is designed around one goal: sustained focus. Clean surfaces, controlled lighting, minimal visual noise. I work on complex, multi-layered projects that require long uninterrupted sessions — the environment is engineered to support that, not compete with it.

It's a simple principle: the less the space demands from your attention, the more attention you can give to the actual work.

## How I Organize Everything — Notion

Notion functions as my central operating system for anything that isn't a direct deliverable. I use it to plan and track ongoing projects, document AI workflows and automation logic, store content frameworks and creative briefs, and log experiments and iteration notes across long-term engagements.

The discipline here matters as much as the tool. When you're managing marketing strategy, automation builds, content production, and client work in parallel, the system that keeps those threads from tangling is what determines whether you scale or stall. Notion is that system for me.

## The Bigger Picture

I work at the intersection of marketing, automation, AI, and content creation — which means on any given day I might be configuring a CRM workflow, color grading a video ad, building a web application, and writing a brand strategy document. The setup described here isn't incidental to that work. It's what makes it possible at the pace and quality level I operate at.

Infrastructure is invisible when it works. That's the point.
