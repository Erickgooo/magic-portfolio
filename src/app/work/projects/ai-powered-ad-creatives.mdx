# Quick Metal Shop & Dakoma Roofing — AI-Powered Ad Creatives

How a lean, one-person production pipeline — built entirely on AI avatars, cloned voices, and rapid editing — replaced traditional video shoots and drove measurable results across two brands, two industries, and two ad objectives.

*Prepared by Erick Mahecha — Growth Marketing Specialist | [erickmahecha.com](http://erickmahecha.com)*

## The Setup

### One Operator. Zero Film Crews. Four Winning Ads.

Every ad in this case study was scripted, rendered, voiced, and edited without a camera, a studio, or a single actor. Nothing was filmed — every scene is AI-generated, and the timeline from brief to published ad is measured in hours, not weeks.

Two production approaches sit under this same umbrella. The two performance ads (01–02) use fully AI-rendered scenes carried by an AI voice-over — no on-camera presenter, just visuals and narration doing the selling. The two flagship creative pieces (03–04) go a step further, putting an AI-generated avatar directly on camera as the spokesperson. Different visual approach, same underlying toolkit — proof the system flexes to the format each objective calls for.

The four ads below are ordered to tell that story: first, the two performance ads that QMS ran to hit hard business goals (direct sales, then qualified leads); then, the two flagship avatar-led creatives that showcase the more advanced side of the AI production process, one for each brand.

## Behind the Scenes

### The AI Production Pipeline

The two flagship creatives (03–04) follow the full four-stage avatar pipeline, run end-to-end by one person:

1. **Avatar creation** — The on-screen presenter is generated in Higgsfield using Nano Banana Pro, producing a photorealistic, brand-appropriate face for each campaign.
2. **Animation** — The static avatar is brought to life with Kling 3.0, adding natural motion, expression, and lip movement.
3. **Voice** — ElevenLabs generates a cloned, natural-sounding voice track matched to the script and the avatar's delivery.
4. **Post-production** — CapCut Pro handles captions, edit pacing, voice-over layering, and final polish before the ad goes live.

The two performance ads (01–02) run a lighter version of the same toolkit: AI-generated scenes, an ElevenLabs voice-over in place of an avatar, and the same CapCut Pro finishing pass — skipping the avatar and animation steps entirely for a faster, more direct-response-friendly build.

No studio time, no talent fees, no shoot scheduling — just a script, a brief, and a handful of AI tools stitched into two repeatable workflows suited to two different jobs: fast performance ads, and flagship brand storytelling.
