## The Challenge

Quick Metal Shop is a laser-cutting and custom metal fabrication business — a category that doesn't naturally lend itself to scroll-stopping social content. There was no video library, no motion footage of installations in progress, and no paid budget. Just a folder of before-and-after photos: empty facades, and the finished laser-cut panels once installed.

The brief I set for myself: turn static "before/after" photography into short-form video content strong enough to compete with entertainment content in the feed — with zero ad spend.

## The Process: A One-Person AI Production Pipeline

Every reel followed the same end-to-end workflow, built and run solo:

1. **Sourcing the hook** — For every video, I selected a "before" photo (bare facade/wall) and an "after" photo (the finished installed piece) from the project archive.
2. **Animating the transformation** — Using Higgsfield's suite with Kling 3.0, I generated a first-frame-to-last-frame animation between the before and after images. This created a cinematic, seamless "reveal" transition as the opening hook of every video — the moment the plain wall becomes the finished architectural piece.
3. **Scripting the voiceover** — Every script was written with Claude 4.6 Sonnet, translating a technical product (laser-cut steel panels) into an emotional, benefit-driven narrative viewers would want to finish watching.
4. **Voice production** — Scripts were converted to natural-sounding voiceover using ElevenLabs.
5. **Editing & pacing** — Final assembly, captions, and pacing were done in CapCut Pro, optimized for short-form retention on Reels.
6. **Distribution** — Every video was cross-posted natively as a Reel to both Instagram and Facebook.

No paid promotion, no stock footage, no film crew — a repeatable content system built from a single designer's laptop.

## Why It Worked

The strategy wasn't "make a nice video" — it was engineer a hook that made an unglamorous product visually satisfying in the first second. The before/after transformation, generated frame-to-frame with Kling 3.0, functioned like a magic trick: viewers stayed to see how the plain wall became the finished piece, and the retention drove the algorithm to push it further.

This project demonstrates a full-stack, AI-augmented growth workflow — from creative direction and scriptwriting to video generation, voice production, and editing — run independently, at zero media cost, for a client with no prior video content.

**Tools used:** Higgsfield (Kling 3.0), Claude 4.6 Sonnet, ElevenLabs, CapCut Pro
